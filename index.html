Dissertation

Conversation-Level Reinforcement Learning

Evergreen · Adaptive Peer Support · 2025 – Present

My research develops reinforcement learning methods for training adaptive conversational agents that can sustain supportive, multi-turn interactions during major life transitions. I work on conversation-level reward optimization, multi-judge LLM evaluation, and integrating passive behavioral sensing from smartphones and wearables — with the goal of making AI systems for health and wellbeing both more personalized and more rigorously evaluated. Current projects include Evergreen, an adaptive peer-support platform for Dartmouth college students, and Therabot, a post-treatment support agent for breast cancer survivors developed at Dartmouth Health.
